**Relativistic interstellar probe concept solving three fundamental paradoxes of the Breakthrough Starshot initiative.**

## The Three Paradoxes & Solutions

| Paradox | Problem | Centaur-Chip Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Braking** | Impossible to flip a sail at 0.2c for retrograde thrust. | **Mirrorless photon ejection** from a hollow-core Möbius accumulator. |
| **Thermal** | The probe would be destroyed by the gigawatt acceleration beam. | **Doppler laser cooling** of the crystal lattice (anti-Stokes cooling). |
| **Communication** | A single chip cannot focus a signal across 4.3 light-years. | **10k-swarm optical VLBI** synchronized with a **Solar Gravity Lens** receiver. |

## Core Technologies

- **Hollow-Core Photonic Crystal Ring with Topological Protection:** Light circulates in an evacuated channel, avoiding dissipative heating.
- **Möbius Strip Geometry:** Compensates radiation pressure, converting destructive force into zero net impulse.
- **In-Situ Stellar Refueling:** The accumulator charges at the target star (Proxima Centauri) just before braking, eliminating extreme storage density requirements.
- **Sacrificial Leading Edge:** A \(1 \mu m\) diamond-like carbon layer that erodes predictably from relativistic interstellar particle impact.

## Energy Density & Survivability

- **Schwinger Limit Analysis:** The accumulator operates at \(10^{19} \text{ J/m}^3\), four orders of magnitude below the electron-positron pair production threshold.
- **Erosion Estimate:** For a typical ISM density of \(0.1 \text{ cm}^{-3}\), erosion is \(\sim 0.7 \mu m\), well within a \(1 \mu m\) sacrificial coating.

## Mission Profiles

- **Scenario A (Return):** Uses a gravitational slingshot around the target star and photon ejection to return to Earth or transmit data.
- **Scenario B (Landing):** Uses accumulated energy for atmospheric braking and a soft landing on an exoplanet.

## Open Engineering Challenges

The concept does not violate known physics but faces significant engineering hurdles explicitly listed in the preprint, including ultra-high vacuum cavity preparation, sub-nanometer swarm positioning at interstellar distances, and bremsstrahlung radiation from the plasma wedge.
